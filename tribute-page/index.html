Chuck Schuldiner

The Godfather of Death Metal

Schuldiner, in red, together with his bandmates.

Timeline of Schuldiner's life

  • 1967 - Born on May 13, on Long Island, New York.
  • 1968 - His family moved to Florida.
  • 1976 - Aged 9, Schuldiner started playing acoustic guitar.
  • 1983 - Chuck formed Mantas, the band which would later become Death. The members were Schuldiner and Rick Rozz as guitarists and Kam Lee as both the drummer and vocalist.
  • 1986 - Moved to Toronto and temporarily joined the Canadian band Slaughter.
  • 1987 - Death released their first album, Scream Bloody Gore.
  • 1988 - With the line-up of former Mantas guitarist Rick Rozz, Terry Butler on bass and Bill Andrews on drums, Schuldiner releases Death's second album, Leprosy
  • 1990 - The album Spiritual Healing is released, with James Murphy replacing Rick Rozz.
  • 1991 - Death's breakthroug album, Human, is released. It features a more technical and progressive style than it's predecessors.
  • 1993 - Death releases Individual Thought Patterns, to much critical acclaim.
  • 1995 - The album Symbolic is released.
  • 1998 - Death releases it's seventh and final album.
  • 2001 - On December 13, Chuck Schuldiner died at the age of 34.
